The Cookie You've Been Missing

Think you know
cookies? Meet
the alfajor.

Imagine two impossibly soft, melt-in-your-mouth shortbread cookies, sandwiched around a cloud of silky dulce de leche — South America's answer to caramel — then finished with a coat of chocolate, a dusting of powdered sugar, or a roll of toasted coconut.

Alfajores are ranked among the Top 5 best cookies in the world by TasteAtlas, beloved across Latin America for centuries. In Uruguay, they are not just a snack — they are a way of life. Uruguayans take dulce de leche seriously, and so do we.

The word "alfajor" comes from the Arabic al-hasú — meaning "the filled one." Brought to the Americas by Spanish colonizers, it found its true home on the Río de la Plata, where small producers and family recipes turned it into an art form.

Our Story

Born in Uruguay.
Baked in California.

La Charrúa started the way most great things do: with homesickness and a recipe. When we moved to California, we brought with us the thing we missed most — the taste of a proper Uruguayan alfajor, made the way families have made them for generations.

Uruguayan alfajores are different. We don't rush. Our dulce de leche is slow-cooked, our dough made fresh, and every alfajor is assembled by hand. We are a small operation, and we intend to stay that way — because quality lives in the details.

In Uruguay, you can find up to 500 different alfajor recipes across the country. We've taken the best of that tradition and brought it to the Bay Area, for anyone who wants to experience what a real alfajor tastes like.
